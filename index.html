Generative AI and Misinformation on Social Platforms - Trends and Examples

With the recent advancements of AI generative tools, we have been seeing an explosion in the volume of videos with AI-generated media on social platforms.
Specifically, AI videos portraying politicians in 2023 have already surpassed all of 2022 by 130%.

The latest biggest trend in the past few weeks is AI Voice Over.
Most creators are using software by the startup ElevenLabs and open source tools such as fakeyou.com.
On January 6, Microsoft unveiled a new tool - Vall-E - a zero-shot text-to-speech model, which can mimic any voice "with only a 3-second enrolled recording of an unseen speaker".

Voice AI - Entertainment

Many of these videos are created for entertainment purposes or as parody.

Voice AI - Misinformation

An increasing pool of videos are using AI voice over to spread misinformation and conspiracy theories.

"America is tyranny and fascism"
"Epstein didn't kill himself"
"Reptilians among us"

Recently, AI voice tools were used to remake the state of the union speech.


Voice AI - Profanity, Drugs, Misogyny

Creators are looking to generate engaging and provocative content and the videos often contain profane language, misogyny and other sensitive content.

ChatGPT - Misinformation

ChatGPT is used to create scripts for misinformation videos. Creators are finding hacks to bypass restrictions and make it repeat false narratives.
